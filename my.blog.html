Islam in the age of ignorance

Guiudance from Surah Al-Kahf Verse 18

Author: Md Shahrukh Khan

If my feelings are ever hurt by a brother or sister in Islam, I use this ayah to help me renew my intentions before I proceed. This applies to situations where the person is not actually harmful, but has hurt or offended me in some way. What I have noticed over the years is that whenever I decided to "stick patiently" with the person and do my best to bypass my own ego for the sake of Allah, I ended up genuinely benefiting in some way, Alhamdullilah. For example, there is a sister I know that can be quite harsh with her words at times, but I know she prays and tries to improve (we all have shortcomings!), but I've tried to "stick patiently" with her and not just remove her from my life, and over the years, her insights on certain matters have truly helped me grow and learn, Alhamdullilah. Even if I didn't always agree, her words have caused me to re-evaluate things and think them through more deeply and come to a better decision. There is another sister who won't respond to me for weeks at a time, which can sometimes feel hurtful - but again, I think of this ayah and I always decide to stick with her, and whenever I do have the opportunity to interact with her, I always benefit a great deal, and I'm grateful that I chose to preserve the bond rather than to destroy it.
